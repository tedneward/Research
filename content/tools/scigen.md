title=SciGen
tags=tool, writing, papers
summary=A program that generates random Computer Science research papers, including graphs, figures, and citations.
~~~~~~

[Website](https://pdos.csail.mit.edu/archive/scigen/) | [Source](https://github.com/strib/scigen)

It uses a hand-written **context-free grammar** to form all elements of the papers. Our aim here is to maximize amusement, rather than coherence.

One useful purpose for such a program is to auto-generate submissions to conferences that you suspect might have very low submission standards. A prime example, which you may recognize from spam in your inbox, is SCI/IIIS and its dozens of co-located conferences (check out the very broad conference description on the [WMSCI 2005](http://www.iiisci.org/sci2005/) website). There's also a list of [known bogus conferences](http://anthony.liekens.net/index.php/Misc/FakeConferences). Using SCIgen to generate submissions for conferences like this gives us pleasure to no end. In fact, one of our papers was accepted to SCI 2005! See [Examples](https://pdos.csail.mit.edu/archive/scigen/#examples) for more details.

We went to WMSCI 2005. Check out the [talks and video](https://pdos.csail.mit.edu/archive/scigen/#talks). You can find more details in our [blog](https://pdos.csail.mit.edu/archive/scigen/blog/).

Also, check out our 10th anniversary celebration project: [SCIpher](https://pdos.csail.mit.edu/archive/scigen/scipher.html)!
